So, here's what I'm doing. I downloaded the Unreal Engine a few months ago, to create the game. The game is a third-person, single player open world set in the city of New Bluecoast, starring Michael Rivard as the protagonist. He works for a scientific company called EARTH. His job: Be a badass, program experimental software and hardware, and steal things. His past few missions were pretty sucky as they involved him stopping cyber-criminal AIs like DevX from spreading viruses and software like ShiftOS and taking over the world. But, his newest mission's pretty amazing. Him and EARTH will work to create the next big thing in technology. Better than VR, Better than augmented reality, Wiping the floor with any PS4, 64GB RAM gaming PC, or Xbox One. What is this amazing thing? Well, EARTH wants to call it the Shifted Dimension. A universe ran and operated completely by one computer, and it can be programmed, explored, and can be entered by any life form in the real universe knowing of it's existence. Of course, with this system being the first of it's kind, there's no golden book to programming such a thing, and there's no machinery capable. But, EARTH is scientific. They can handle inventing the machinery and programming. All Michael has to do is follow their orders, stealing machinery from various New Bluecoast companies, meeting friends and enemies along the way, and looking like a badass doing so. Once the Shifted Dimension is built, Michael is the test subject. He must go into the Shifted Dimension and program even more stuff for it... but he soon discovers that EARTH isn't what he thought they were. I mean, how would you explain them creating self-replicating software, essentially creating ShiftOS, the most controversial virus ever, responsible for causing trillions of dollars in damage just from hard drive formats? How would you explain them telling Michael to steal from billion dollar companies just to build a universe of a large scale? Michael finds out the truth about EARTH, and they know it. Now it's time for Michael to get his revenge, and destroy EARTH once and for all, gaining complete control of the Shifted Dimension in the process.

So, yeah... just a little bit of info about a project I'm making. Of course, this ain't no VB script-kiddie bullcrap, so it's going to be INCREDIBLY tough to create on my own. If anyone's interested in helping out, let me know. I'm not putting any money into this, as I'm distributing the game for free. Anything will help. Music, story, 3D models, textures, voices, sound effects, UE4 Blueprints, anything.

Re: A little project I'm working on... - Michael - 08-17-2015

Yes. I've said countless amounts of time, in countless chats, heck, even the UE4 forums, that I'm NOT MAKING a GTA V-level game, or anything considered even a AA game. It's for me to learn, and to experiment. Heck, the open-world part is fucking easy. Switch on World Composition in a blank level, then put each tile of the world in a separate level, then World Composition'll load 'em up for you, then you can arrange the tiles to form a world map which can be edited from within the persistent level. Bam, you made an Open World. Now, all you need to do is add RPG elements, some missions, and a playable character pawn. The character pawn's easy, because when creating a project, you could easily choose between many templates, whether it be 2D, 3D, First Person, Third Person, Driving, lots.

As for high-quality trees and grass, just use the Kite Demo package. It's only 6.3 GB and will really speed up the process of creating forests and scenic areas, that is if you can craft the Blueprints necessary to handle the procedural generation, which shouldn't be difficult. Hop in the Experimental section of the Engine Preferences and you'll find some tools that'll do this for you. Fun fact: These tools were used at either GDC 2014 or GDC 2015 (if that happened, if it hasn't yet than it was 2014) to show off Open World development in UE4? Yeah. There's a reason they're in the experimental section.

For the city, hop in Blender, or your favorite modelling software, and stop being lazy and practice modelling and texturing until you've made a few houses, shops, skyscrapers, offices, nuke plants factories. etc, and a few street props like lamps, benches, etc, and some road segments. Then, piece 'em up all together in the UE4 world, and bam. Open world with a playable character and a city done.

Now let's add some missions and cutscenes. Go experiment with Blueprints and Matinee, until you've made something cool. Be sure to watch some of the UE4 tutorials by people like Tesla Dev, and even Epic Games themselves.

All done? Great, you made an RPG. Now you can play around with more Blueprint and see if you can add driveable cars like in GTA.

GTA-like game checklist:
- Open world: CHECK!
- Third person or first person character: CHECK!
- Scenic areas (mountains, forests, etc): CHECK!
- Driveable cars: CHECK!
- RPG-like non-linear misssions: CHECK!

There, you've successfully made a ghetto GTA III with next-gen graphics. Add a second dimension and a few super powers and some more missions and the ability to program it, and you have a ghetto Shifted Dimension.

Oh, and if you need a headstart on modelling the city, there are various sites giving away free models like streetlamps and bridges. You'll just have to look around for models that are either in .fbx or models that can safely be imported into Blender than exported into .fbx files. Oh, and Blender's scaling units are in centimeters, and UE4's are in meters, so when exporting your model, set the Scale to x100 before exporting so your model shows up the right size in UE4, otherwise your character will be Godzilla and can move around and destroy your city just by walking forward. Not a bad game, but we're not going for that.
