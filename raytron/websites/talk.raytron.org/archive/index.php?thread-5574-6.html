Jelle Wrote:Holy fuck, 16GB RAM? damn

If we're being realistic, in the modern world, 8 gbs of RAM is starting to show it's limitations, especially for gamers, video producers, and CAD artists. Most people who are young these days are doing one of those 3, so naturally 8 gbs is definitely showing it's limits. I myself am starting to max out this 8 gb pc, and am highly considering chucking another 8 GB stick in here for 16 GB (despite the fact that this PC can go up to 32 GB, THAT'S overkill). Even though I don't play multiple games simultaneously, it is hard playing some more recent games with 8 GB of RAM. Cities Skylines runs good now that I have my GPU, but once you get really far in, 8 GB will not cut it.

4 GB was the standard for quite some time, but it seems that 8 gbs is becoming the standard, and like I said even that is showing it's limitations for gamers, video producers, and cad artists. One might argue that it's all in the GPU, but truth is ram and processor matter too in Gaming and CAD especially. It's not as big of a deal in video work, but rendering can take awhile if you have a shitty processor and shitty amount of ram.
