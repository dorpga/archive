Re: Would an Intel Xeon be good for gaming?

by Insyd3 » Wed Jul 02, 2014 4:33 am

You don't need anything close to the power of a workstation machine to get max graphics on BF4.

A Xenon processor is like comparing a 2010 Honda to a 2012 Lamborghini. While the honda will perform just fine for regular driving the Lamborghini will only be able to use that extra performance in certain conditions and is just for eye candy or status symbol.

I'm not saying that a workstation isn't capable of gaming however it is just workstation computers are for specialised use, they will run games perfectly however it's not worth the investment since you are paying a lot more for power that you aren't going to use.

A Xenon is not a sensible solution for what you are looking at, gaming PC's are little more than PC's built with components that a user chooses rather than the low end crap OEM's might install.

If you are going to be gaming I suggest the latest generation of i5 processors as an i7 doesn't use the addition hyperthreaded cores to it's full benefit and the i7 only realistically performs better at video rendering.

Re: Would an Intel Xeon be good for gaming?

by Insyd3 » Thu Jul 03, 2014 12:09 am

Honestly most games today don't even take advantage of an i7's virtual cores properly, you would only see a benefit in areas like video rendering. I chose an i5 but I just leave my computer to render videos which doesn't really take all that long (roughly 10 mins). Anything higher than a quad core CPU is just overkill although there are some games that are more CPU and RAM intensive than others (Civilization 5 requires a Good CPU and good amount of ram to load all the AI's). other than that if you aren't going to take full advantage of it it's not really worth it.

My gaming rig runs an i5 and it plays all games above at roughly 70-90 fps depends on the game, it also runs minecraft at nearly 400fps. I also run a GTX 670 for my graphics card so don't flush all your money into a good CPU if you are looking to do some gaming.
