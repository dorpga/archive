How we have come to the PS4 and Shitbox One.

by MetroHaterx54x2 » Sat May 31, 2014 5:06 am

In 2014, we have both 8th gen consoles, that are in fact very similar.
Both pack 8-core Semi-custom AMD Jaguar APUs, with 8 GB of RAM.

However, that is the end of the similarity, the PS4 is sleek and modern, while you can easily confuse the Xbox one for a cable box.
The PS4 seems to be aimed at the more game playing people, and the Xbox one at the people who spend 90% of their time watching TV.
The PS4 also packs 8 GB of GDDR5 memory, something you cannot find even on an AMD R9 290X or GTX 780 Ti, while the XBOX ONE has 8 GB of slower DDR3 memory, not to mention the XBOX one has the worthless kinect, which is the reason for the $499 price point (or $399 without the kinect) while the PS4 at $399 as well. The CPU and GPU compare very closely, the XBOX ONE at 1.75 GHz (2 cores reserved for background services) and PS4 at 1.6 GHz (all cores available, turbo boost to unknown speed, second ultra-low-power CPU reserved for background services), same going for GPUs, PS4 having 18 CUs, 1152 shaders at 800 MHz, and XBOX ONE with 12 CUs and 768 shaders at 853 MHz.
While GPU clock speed still carries some weight, it isn't enough to make up that 50% CU decrease on the XBOX ONE.
To add to that, 2% of the GPU power in the XBOX One is reserved for kinect.
However, don't forget the doomed Wii U. Only sporting a sad Triple-core at 1.3 GHz, 2 GB RAM (eww), and a tiny amount of storage, it is on par with last gen consoles from a performance perspective.


However, the playstation brand still existed 14 years ago, and their latest console then was the Playstation 2, the best selling game console of all time that finally got discontinued on January 4th, 2013, meaning it lasted in both the 6th gen and 7th gen.
Microsoft had released the XBOX in 2001, the same year of the launch of the popular Windows XP.
Going back even further, in 1994/1995 the original PlayStation launched, changing the gaming industry forever, with it's CD format glory,

However, this wasn't the first sight of the playstation, in 1991 Sony unveiled the "Play Station" with the intent of playing SNES and SNES-CD games, however shortly after Nintendo broke up with Sony when trying to make a CD addon for the SNES. Sony didn't scrap the Play Station however, they worked on it to make it CD only and make it have it's own format, and that was how the playstation came to be, with the first part just comparing PS4 to Xbox one.
