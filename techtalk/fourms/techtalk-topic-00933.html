5 Linux Apps You Didn't Know You Needed

Some Linux apps are ubiquitous. X.Org and GNU are suites of software taken for granted on modern Linux desktops. A window manager like i3 or OpenBox or my favorite - Compiz - is expected too but there are many WMs out there.

Anyways, let's look at the apps you may not have, but you really need.

1. Pulseaudio
Still running ALSA+dmix? Get your integrated sound chip out of the gutter. USB headsets, easy device swtiching, and all that jazz can be done - with Pulseaudio.

2. pavucontrol
If you're using Pulseaudio you can't dent that pavucontrol is the WAY TO GO when it comes to a graphical interface for your sound settings. Just download Pulseaudio and pavucontrol together in the same command.

3. mpg123
You've downloaded the new episode of Hello Internet. You type carefully: "aplay ./21.mp3". But that gives you horrible high pitched screeching, and OH MY GOD MY EARS ARE BLEEDING TURN THIS OFF NOW PLEAAAASSEEE!!!!!! This isn't Brady Haran, or CGP Grey, it's horrifying! How DO you play MP3s correctly from the commandline? Well, fine sir, get mpg123. It's as simple as that. Then just run "mpg123 ./21.mp3" to hear that podcast.

4. compiz-boxmenu
Running Compiz? Ditch XFCE, KDE, Unity, whatever you're using, because there's a much more lightweight alternative. If you use Compiz's desktop feature (I'm assuming nitrogen won't work) just use compiz-boxmenu. It's like a compositing OpenBox...or something.

5. Steam
I know that you're thinking "What the hell everybody has steam". But they don't. On Linux, many gamers opt to just download crap from the package manager, which is NOT how things work. Games are more art than simply a utility program and they deserve to cost money, and you're going to find great games like Portal on here. So if you're still using the package manager to download games you're missing out on the whole world of gaming.

Also something you should know...
If you have a mouse that can do a middle click, you can select text in any application, and middle click somewhere else to paste it there. It's really simple and awesome.
