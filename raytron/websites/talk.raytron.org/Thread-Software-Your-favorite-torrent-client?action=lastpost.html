Hold up a minute, there's a lot of cancer here. Let me talk about bandwidth limitation over ping on single thread downloads for a second. I asked KomputerKid what mirror he uses and what the ping to that mirror is. His mirror gains a ping of approximately 20ms. With this in mind, let's calculate the maximum throughput for his average of 20ms ping, assuming a 64k jumbo frame size.
524288/0.020=26,214,400 bits per second. 26.2mbps. Converting that into a real figure such as MiB/s, we get 3.12MiB/s. That's a pretty shit figure for a single thread download speed on a fast connection. Granted there's the argument of using download managers or browsers with multithread downloading, but we still have real world network performance such as multithread connections going through routing etc to take into account, as two threads does not double the throughput. Should KomputerKid want to take advantage of a faster internet connection, torrenting is a great way of doing it since there are plenty of P2P seeds for *nix images. I know from experience on using a connection capable of 380mbps. Of course with mirrors as well, there's the potential for issues and load balancing settings to be used, or even in some cases where there is a limit on the number of concurrent threads to a server. There's a reason for everything. Protip on the internet is to not talk shit unless you know your shit.
So yeah, not everybody hasn't just HTTP/FTP'd an ISO for the past 2 years.
</offtopic>
