Fancy Mario!?

by maxell » Thu Sep 25, 2014 2:07 am

If you have ever played a NES (or Famicom) multicart you'll know about the multicart variants they use. Stuff like Moon Mario, Fancy Mario, and Long Live Mario.

Fancy mario is really challenging and it makes SMB more of a puzzle game. One day I hope to pick up a multicart just for it.

Basically, you see what's in front of you behind you. But don't be fooled by the enemies, they still can hurt you. You'll also have to memorize the landscape because when a part of the level scrolls to the left half of the screen you don't see it anymore, you only see what's in front of you. Here's some guys I've never heard of fooling around with a Famiclone that has Fancy Mario on it.

XSerpentGotham83 wrote:So these things were like "mods" or something...?


Essentially. The more rudimentary ones (like Fancy Mario) apply modifications akin to Game Genie code(s). They just temporarily change a byte in the program's binary.

Of course there are ROM hacks which is a different set of tricks entirely, and involves permanently changing the actual contents of the ROM chip.
