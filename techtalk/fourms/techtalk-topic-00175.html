Well, it's not really a screen of death per se, it's more like a screen of error. Not all BSODs are going to wreck your system, and more often than not actually protect your files from being damaged.

I got one when I was like 3 on some Windows XP box. The whole OS somehow got erased from the machine, although I forget how or why.

I haven't gotten a BSOD since, mainly because of my minimal Windows usage.
