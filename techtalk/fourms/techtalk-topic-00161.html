I do and I don't agree because:
What drives the economy is not profit, or a company gaining control for a long time. It's competition. And the reason people work, think, and innovate is to beat the competition at their goals, and so they get money instead of the competition. And every company has flaws, so there will be new competition that fixes them (or hopes to) and people will work harder for that company. There are two possible outcomes of your proposal:

1. The company would simply obliterate any mention of competition and therefore have a monopoly, giving people no real incentive to work towards beating anyone, and giving customers no real change, because it's not like they have any reason to improve.

2. The company quickly cannibalizes itself because people know that they are blocking data which is perfectly legal and simply opinionated, and people do switch.

All in all, it's just another brick in the wall why should people be paying more for an inferior product just because some company feels like being a douche today?
