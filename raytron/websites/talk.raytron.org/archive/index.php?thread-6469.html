I'm guessing you just got a defective SSD. If you want to test it to make sure, you could try burning a copy of Hiren's BootCD and using one of the utilities on it to check the SSD just to make sure the drives bad.

Now, because you just got this last week, what I would try to do is go back to the place you bought this off of and see if they will refund you. They'll probably give you the choice of either exchanging your SSD with another one or giving your money back outright (really depends on the business though).

What I would do is just take the refund (if the place you bought it from even gives you one) and go for a Crucial or Samsung SSD as they are both pretty reliable SSD brands (nothing wrong with SanDisk though, I've just never had great luck with their products).

If you can't for some reason get a refund through the place you bought it for whatever reason, contact SanDisk and have it fixed though your drives warranty. They will probably send you a refurb unit, though it might take you a little bit to get it if they make you wait until they receive the defective SSD before they ship your replacement. Save going though that hassle as a last resort though.
