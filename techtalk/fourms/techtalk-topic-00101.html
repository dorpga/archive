Re: Ultimate Motherboard?

by maxell » Sun May 25, 2014 7:57 am

- Turbo button functionality to dynamically change the clock speed of the CPU, maybe via a potentiometer (I.e. a dial) on the front of the case.
- The ability to drop in multiple CPU pin layouts through a mechanically changeable CPU socket
- The BIOS would be open source and have the ability to recognize file systems
- The BIOS would, in fact, be a partition on a SATA SSD.
- The BIOS would be GRUB 3
- There would be ISA, PCI, AGP, PCI-E, PCMCIA, USB, Thunderbolt, FireWire, and two-tin-cans-connected-by-a-string support.
- There would be an on-board Tandy 3-voice
- The board itself would be like a waterbed, with super cold dry ice and water being pumped into it.

Re: Ultimate Motherboard?

by Ham62 » Mon May 26, 2014 1:13 am

The ultimate motherboard would have:

-Support for a turbo switch
-Support for a reset switch
-Two CPU sockets for 2x the power!
-6 RAM slots like on my 486 systems so you could load up on that DDR2 RAM
-Inboard sound chip that is Soundblaster compatible with that SB16 style midi playback
-5 PCI slots for my extra cards
-Inboard 100MB/s network chipset
-A graphics card slot
-Inboard IDE controller card with support for regular HDDs, floppy drives, CD-ROM, DVD, and laptop HDDs.
-Flashable BIOS for quick and easy updates

I think this is everything I would like it to have.
Just because something is old doesn't mean it's useless, you just need to find more creative ways to use it!
