Re: Is all the hate gone now?

by MajorSky17 » Fri May 30, 2014 4:47 pm

I don't think all the hate has gone, but I think a lot of the "I tried using Windows 8 once/twice/three times and gave up. I hate it!!!" has gone as more people have encountered it/had it forced upon them. Personally I never found it that bad in terms of usability - avoidance of the Start menu due to it not existing in 8.x did take me a while to get my head around, but once I'd relearnt the steps needed to get to Control Panel, etc. I found it much more productive to use.

My main concern with 8.x is that it actually seems, relatively, incredibly unstable to me compared with 7. It has broken every single one of my computers at least once (including my Surface Pro) - usually all through buggy updates - and whilst I had to reinstall in on my Surface Pro (seems backward to try running anything else on it), I opted to go back to 7 on my laptop and netbook. I did reinstall 8.1 on my desktop, which it subsequently broke again recently. So now I'm back with 7 on that too. When I was using 8.x, I must have gotten about 5 different kinds of BSOD within a few months. Now that I'm back with 7, as with before, I have not had any. Any. The only things I miss are the ribbon interface in Explorer and the faster boot times, but I can live without those in exchange for higher reliability. I just haven't got time to recover all my data for a third time if 8.x breaks my PC again.
