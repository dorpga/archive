At what point does hacking become illegal? (Debate)

by KomputerKid » Sat May 24, 2014 11:28 pm

At what point do you think the process of hacking becomes illegal, and the "hacker" should be put on trial. I think if you are accessing somebodies system and ruining it for "fun" or personal gain, then that is illegal. However if you are a security specialist (Or "White Hat hacker") and are employed in testing security of various websites, with their written consent, then it is legal. I also think it is good to know how to hack, as you can use that to thwart any attacks made by another hacker. For instance, my dad setup a security system for his Macbook. He then wanted to test it, so he decided to hack himself in order to find out what might need to be changed, or what is working. Would you consider hacking yourself on your own network illegal? What if you hack yourself on somebody else's network? Would it be legal then? Where do you guys define hacking as illegal?

Hacking yourself or your company's servers with permission (whitehat hacker) is legal. Classifying permissible remote desktop as legal hacking is what I would say. However, hacking others without consent as to steal information should be illegal. Writing malware to hack should be illegal, although in 2001, it wasn't in Manila. Overall, hacking others without consent is the only illegal practice of hacking--permissible remote desktop, hacking to fix flaws, or testing a security system are legal.
