esc1010 wrote:I tried Intel for gaming and the i7 on my Ubuntu laptop ran Minecraft at a sad 19 fps and a light weight game called tanki online ran at 33 fps which is bearable. There is also a game called Plague Inc: Evolved which ran better at around 55 fps.


You know it's probably not your processor. That's probably your video card. In terms of gaming, the processor doesn't really do shit. It just helps to have a decent core amount, and a good clock speed. The thing that really matters in gaming though is your video card. I'm gonna guess you have a shitty one.

Here's an example. My dell computer has a celeron processor. The video card is an Intel HD 4400. Yet Minecraft runs okay on it (60 + FPS) despite having a celeron.

My desktop PC has an Intel Pentium 4 and a GTX 650 and Minecraft runs at like still 50 or so FPS on default settings. This is because of the fact that the pc has a good video card.
